positive benefits

Pedestrian-Friendly Community

Mobile food services offer many benefits to local residents including greater street vitality, safety (with more “eyes on the street”), pedestrian‑friendly environments and neighborhood interaction. Instead of relying on vehicles to travel to physical restaurants, food trucks are a great alternative.

Affordable, Convenient

There are tons of affordable and convenient food options to satisfy a range of hunger. Usually by taking a stroll through a park (or parking lot!), or seeking them out through food trucks festivals that support local brands.

Employment Opportunities

The lower barriers to entry to with low start‑up costs make this business model more accessible middle‑class entrepreneurs, new Canadians and aspiring chefs. Mobile food services can also help fill the void in areas that are not well‑served by restaurants and have positive impacts on other fixed businesses by bringing customers to a particular area.

Simulate Demand for B and M

Rather than being “unfair competition”, mobile food services and restaurants largely reflect two different business models with different levels of investments and services.ome evidence suggests that mobile food services may be stimulating demand in the food service market by attracting new customers that would not have purchased food at all were it not for the food trucks.
